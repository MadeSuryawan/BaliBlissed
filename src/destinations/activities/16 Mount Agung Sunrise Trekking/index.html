Conquer Mount Agung: Bali's Ultimate Sunrise Trek

Challenge yourself on Bali's highest peak (3,142m) and witness an unforgettable sunrise from the roof of the island. (For experienced hikers only).

Trek to the Summit of Mount Agung

Location

Mount Agung

View on Google map

Difficulty

Very Challenging (Experienced Hikers)

Duration

Approx. 10-12 hours (incl. trek & transfers)

Are you an experienced hiker seeking the ultimate challenge in Bali? Look no further than Mount Agung, the island's highest and most sacred volcano. Standing tall at 3,142 meters (10,308 feet), this demanding trek rewards those who reach the summit with truly breathtaking, panoramic sunrise views that stretch across Bali, potentially revealing neighboring islands like Lombok on clear days.

This is not a leisurely stroll; the ascent involves significant vertical inclines and requires excellent physical condition and prior hiking experience. Starting in the dead of night, you'll navigate challenging terrain under the stars with our expert local guides. As you climb higher, feel the air cool and the anticipation build. Reaching the summit as the first light breaks is an incredibly rewarding experience, offering unparalleled photo opportunities and a profound sense of accomplishment.

Important Note: This trek is significantly more difficult than Mount Batur due to its steepness and duration. It is strictly recommended for fit and experienced hikers. If you prefer a less strenuous but still beautiful sunrise hike, we highly recommend our Mount Batur Sunrise Trekking Tour .

Important Considerations:

  • Experience Level: This trek is physically demanding with steep inclines and is suitable only for experienced hikers in good physical condition.
  • Alternative: For a less challenging sunrise hike, consider the Mount Batur trek .
  • Starting Points: Treks typically start from either Pasar Agung Temple (shorter, steeper) or Besakih Temple (longer). Your chosen route may affect pickup time and duration.
  • Pickup Time: Pickup is typically late the night *before* your chosen participation date (e.g., pickup on Dec 19th night for a Dec 20th trek). Please confirm details upon booking.
  • What to Bring: Sturdy hiking boots, warm layers (it gets cold!), windproof jacket, hat, gloves, sunscreen, camera, personal snacks, and sufficient water.
