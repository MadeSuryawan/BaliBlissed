Nestled along Bali's northeastern coastline, Amed offers one of the island's most spectacular underwater experiences—a pristine marine sanctuary where vibrant coral gardens and diverse aquatic life thrive just meters from the volcanic black sand shores. Our exclusive daytrip gives you the freedom to choose your preferred way to explore this underwater paradise: either immerse yourself completely with a guided scuba diving adventure (suitable for both certified divers and beginners) or enjoy the colorful marine ecosystem from the surface with our premium snorkeling experience.

For diving enthusiasts and those eager to try scuba for the first time, our PADI-certified instructors will guide you through Amed's world-renowned dive sites, including the spectacular USS Liberty Shipwreck and the vibrant Japanese Wreck. Beginners will receive comprehensive instruction and can experience the thrill of breathing underwater in shallow, controlled conditions before exploring deeper. Certified divers can immediately enjoy guided dives tailored to their experience level, discovering Amed's underwater treasures including colorful reef systems, fascinating macro life, and the occasional visit from larger pelagic species like manta rays and reef sharks.

If you prefer to stay closer to the surface, our snorkeling option provides an equally mesmerizing experience without the need for diving certification or equipment training. Float effortlessly above thriving coral gardens where schools of tropical fish create living rainbows beneath you. Our experienced guides know exactly where to find the most vibrant reefs and interesting marine creatures, ensuring even snorkelers get to witness the full splendor of Amed's underwater ecosystem. Both options include high-quality equipment, expert guidance, and the opportunity to capture your underwater adventure with optional underwater photography services. After your marine exploration, enjoy a delicious beachside lunch featuring fresh local cuisine while you share stories of your underwater discoveries.

Tip: For the best underwater visibility, morning sessions are recommended. Don't forget to bring a change of clothes, sunscreen, and a towel. If you're prone to seasickness, consider taking medication before the trip. Underwater cameras are available for rent, or you can bring your own waterproof camera to capture the incredible marine life. Remember that touching or standing on coral is harmful to these delicate ecosystems—our guides will ensure you practice responsible tourism.
